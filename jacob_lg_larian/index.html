How do you encourage players to create not just character sheets, but actual characters for a D&D game? For my games, I've homebrewed additional mechanics unique to each playable race that encourage players to create dynamic characters with motivations and connections to the world. Errant
A narrative puzzle platformer
Errant is a puzzle platformer where you play as an amnesiac knight stuck in the realm of an enigmatic Fey. The Fey will barter for your lost memories in exchange for unlocking new sections of her twisting halls. However, each memory exchanged will also alter the letter that is your only clue to how you ended up here. Can you escape this labyrinth? What will you choose remember from before?
